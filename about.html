Our Story

About The Church

Soul Harvest Kiembeni started on the 28th of April 2008 as a fellowship of 7 believers. The fellowship took place in a school classroom where the members would preach in turns since there was no Pastor. At that time, the fellowship operated under Soul Harvest Ministries International led by Apostle Josephat Ndumbu.

Apostle Josephat Ndumbu, the Ministry leader, ordained Gilbert Ruwa as the Pastor of Soul Harvest Kiembeni on 3rd of August 2008; Four months after its inception.

A year after its inception, the church had grown to 70 members. This tremendous growth was attributed to one evangelism & open-air crusades by the church. The church has continued to grow ever since to more than 400 members today.

Church Developments

In 2010, The Church was able to buy a piece of land measuring 70ft by 140ft in Bombo area. The building of the church began in 2011 and by 2013 we moved from the classroom to the unfinished building and conducted our services from there.

The church members were committed to completing the church building and by 2016 the church construction was completed. It was officially dedicated by Apostle Josephat Ndumbu - Founder and Chairman of Soul Harvest Ministries Intl.

In 2016, Missionary Jane Steel from the UK blessed the church with Power crusade equipment I.e PA system & a truck. She also assisted in the acquisition of more land around the church and today the church sits on almost an acre of land.

Furthermore, we have invested in training ministers to help us in achieving our mandate and vision. Our Mandate is dividing an inheritance to God's children (Joshua 1:6). Our Vision is to become a center where quality disciples whose lives positively impact others and influence destinies (Isaiah 2:2-3)

In 2020, we adopted the church in Timboni, Kilifi County and sent Pastor Japhet (our former assistant Pastor) to lead it. In April 2022, we started a satellite church in Silaloni Village, Kwale County and God has been faithful.

Future Ministry and Development Plans

In line with our vision of producing quality disciples, we intend to plant two more churches by the end of 2023, one in Mombasa and another in Kilifi County.

We also trust the Lord for International doors to open. Through our able Bishop, we have been able to penetrate South Africa and Zambia where he has ministered in conferences several times. By God's grace, we shall be launching a School of Ministry in South Africa and Seychelles. Our vision bearer, Bishop Gilbert Ruwa, holds dear that God wants us to equip Gospel ministries around the world who will also equip others in their respective nations.

Development wise, we plan to build a school within the church as a way of giving back to the community. We also aspire to build a vocational Training College which will also be necessary for the empowerment of the Community.
